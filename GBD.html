The Hidden Drop-off: Cosigner Funnel
🔍
Cosigner Abandonment — The Invisible Funnel Inside the Funnel
Site-Verified Finding
What the site says
GradBridge's own site states: "Most students will need a creditworthy cosigner to qualify — someone like a parent, guardian, or trusted adult."

This means every application involves two separate people completing a form. The cosigner is a second conversion point — and it's entirely invisible.
Where the drop-off likely lives
894 apps completed
100%
↓ Cosigner required — student must recruit & wait
~644 cosigners start
~72%
↓ Cosigner abandons — not CampusDoor's problem to fix
547 approved
61.2%
Cosigner abandonment is estimated — not currently measurable. This is the problem.
What to do about it
Instrument it first. Request cosigner start/abandon events from CampusDoor via their API or webhook — even a simple email notification when a cosigner drops.
Facebook retargeting. Parents (35–55yr) who visited but didn't complete a cosigner form. Facebook is exactly the right channel for this audience.
Cosigner nurture email. A separate HubSpot sequence triggered when a cosigner is invited but hasn't completed. GradBridge controls this; CampusDoor doesn't.
